Design Insight

``Exploratory Data Analysis is detective work'.' John Tukey frames EDA as a body of tools and understanding. Armed with both, statisticians and data scientists have made bold strides in solving real problems. Engineers are traditionally armed with a great deal of understanding about physical systems. However, an engineering analogue for exploratory tools does not exist.

I develop tools to help engineers probe systems for explainable, exploitable understanding -- Design Insight.

I have developed techniques for extracting qualitative understanding in shape design problems, leveraging modern dimension reduction strategies. My cross-disciplinary background in engineering and statistics has given me a unique view into problems, and has enabled me to develop an automated detection procedure for lurking variables.

Managing Uncertainty

Uncertainty proliferates in complex engineering systems. Legal and cost constraints prevent full-scale tests, necessitating modeling assumptions and extrapolatory computations. However, historical engineering practices -- such as the safety factor approach -- treat uncertainty by making it irrelevant. These practices add excessive margin, and fail to identify the underlying salient uncertainties. 

I work to identify such failure points in the classical engineering practice, and update them with modern procedures which address the underlying uncertainties.
